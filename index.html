Transforming Irrigation in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa

A landmark initiative by the Government of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa to enhance agricultural productivity, improve water management, and uplift rural communities through modern irrigation infrastructure.

Project Overview GIS Portal

Project Overview

Water Resources Development Project is a transformative initiative enhancing irrigation infrastructure in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. It includes the Pehur Main Canal (PMC) in Swabi District and the Mulkhow irrigation system, aiming to address water scarcity and boost agricultural productivity through efficient water distribution.

Primary Objectives

  • Provide reliable irrigation to over 53,228 acres of farmland
  • Reduce water wastage through modern channel design
  • Enhance agricultural productivity and farmer income
  • Implement sustainable water management practices
  • Develop climate-resilient irrigation infrastructure

Project Timeline

Project Initiation

January 2024

Feasibility studies, initial planning, and stakeholder consultations.

Design Finalization

June 2024

Engineering design completion and environmental impact assessment.

Construction Phase 1

September 2024 - March 2025

Earthworks and main canal construction in the northern sector.

Construction Phase 2

April 2025 - June 2025

Branch canals and distribution network development.

Testing & Commissioning

June 2025 - August 2025

System testing, water flow trials, and quality assurance.

Full Operation

December 2025 Onward

Operational commencement and farmer onboarding.
